Step 2. Evaluating your offer

Asking yourself these questions will help you understand how accepting this offer can impact your ability to pay back your student debt and impact your financial future.

Am I about to take out too many loans?

There are two factors that make up your debt burden—your loan payment and your salary. The general rule of thumb is that your student loan payment shouldn’t be more than 14% of your monthly income.

If your debt burden is too high, it increases the chances of defaulting on your loan or not being able to pay for other necessities, like health insurance. Since it’s difficult to predict or actively increase your future salary, the best way to lower your debt burden is to reduce the amount of student loans you take out.

Step 3. You have options

Rest assured that there are things you can do if you are uncertain about taking the next step. Here are some choices you can make that may help you improve your financial future.

Maximize all available grants and scholarships.

Consider applying for additional scholarships and grants, which provide money you don't have to repay. By increasing the amount of this type of aid, you can decrease the overall amount of debt you have to borrow—and consequently pay back.

Reduce your living costs.

Living at home or finding cheaper off-campus housing can reduce the cost of attendance, meaning you'll need to borrow less money overall.

Consider a different program with better outcomes.

Salaries are often influenced by the degree you earn. If the projected salary of your program is far below the national average, you might consider a different program with a higher projected earning potential.

Explore other schools that have similar programs.

Review your total cost of attendance and determine if a less expensive school option (such as a community college) might also meet your educational needs.

Ask if credits from your school transfer to degrees earned at other schools.

In the case you decide to finish your education somewhere else, it’s important to know if the hard work you’ve put into your degree will be honored at another school.

What you can do

Using some of the strategies outlined above in the evaluation, try adjusting some of the offer amounts in the tool. For instance, you can try reducing the amount of federal or private loans to see how it affects your overall debt.

You can also research schools to compare things like graduation rate and projected salary.
